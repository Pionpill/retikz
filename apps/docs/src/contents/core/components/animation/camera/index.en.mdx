Camera and stagger are the two animations that act **on the whole, not on a single element's look**: `cameraTo` animates the scene's viewbox, while `stagger` adjusts the start times of a group of animations. For the common options and the full preset table, see the [Overview](/core/components/animation).

## Camera

<ComponentPreview name="camera-to" size="md" />

`cameraTo({ from, to })`: animates the scene's viewbox `[x, y, w, h]` for pan / zoom. Attach it to `<Layout animations>` (the camera belongs to the scene root, not an element); both endpoints are explicit.

## Stagger

<ComponentPreview name="stagger" size="sm" />

`stagger(tracks, step)`: adds increasing `delay` across a group (0, step, 2×step…) for a one-by-one entrance. It choreographs the start times of the element animations ([Intro](/core/components/animation/entrance) / [Emphasis & Loop](/core/components/animation/emphasis)); it doesn't change any single animation's shape.
