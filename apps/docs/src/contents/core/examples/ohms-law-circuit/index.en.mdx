## Introduction

This page builds a textbook-style Ohm's law experiment circuit while showing how [Layout](/core/components/layout/overview), [Scope](/core/components/layout/scope), [Node](/core/components/node/overview), [Draw](/core/components/draw/overview), and the [Shape Registry](/core/reference/extending/shape-registry) work together.

The meters, battery, switch, resistors, and rheostat are all custom shapes that wire up through fixed terminals. The figure is built in "shapes first, then place, then wire and label" order.

## Process

### Step 1: Build the meter as a custom shape

The voltmeter and ammeter are a round meter head with a lead on each side, wiring only at the left/right terminals (the same idea as the "semantic terminals: diode" in the [Shape Registry](/core/reference/extending/shape-registry)). Build it as a custom `circuit-meter` shape and flatten two of them (A / V) to start.

<ComponentPreview
  name="circuit-01-meters"
  sourceFiles={['circuit-01-meters.meter.tsx']}
  size="xs"
/>

### Step 2: Flatten the remaining custom symbols

The battery, switch, resistor, and rheostat are not built into core either, so they are custom shapes in the [Shape Registry](/core/reference/extending/shape-registry), collected in `circuitShapes.tsx`. They each expose only left-right terminals (the battery's long plate is the positive on the right, the short plate the negative on the left); the plates, contacts, rectangles, and arrow are all drawn inside the shape.

<ComponentPreview
  name="ohms-law-circuit-02-custom-shapes"
  sourceFiles={['circuitShapes.tsx']}
  size="xs"
/>

### Step 3: Place the elements on the circuit

Place the battery, switch, ammeter, and rheostat in their final positions — the resistors and voltmeters are left out for now, to be assembled in the next step. The battery stands on the left (rotated 90°), the switch and ammeter sit on the top row, and the rheostat on the bottom row.

<ComponentPreview
  name="ohms-law-circuit-03-placement"
  sourceFiles={['circuitShapes.tsx', 'circuit-01-meters.meter.tsx']}
  size="sm"
/>

### Step 4: Compose and reuse the measurement unit with Scope

A resistor plus a voltmeter right below it, with both ends joined by elbow polylines, all wrapped in one [Scope](/core/components/layout/scope) — that is a "measurement unit". The second one is not rewritten: a `Scope` `translate` duplicates it, changing only the id prefix. The diff highlights exactly these two newly added units.

<ComponentPreview
  name="ohms-law-circuit-04-measurement-cells"

### Step 5: Wire a closed loop with elbow connectors

Once the elements are placed, use [Draw](/core/components/draw/overview) to connect them in order: battery top → switch → ammeter → R1 → down the right side → R2 → rheostat → back to the battery bottom. The wires run as right-angle elbows (`|- -|`) and reference element terminals instead of guessed coordinates.

/>

### Step 6: Label the components

Finish with the `E`, `S`, `R1` / `R2`, `U1` / `U2`, and rheostat-name text labels and the figure reads clearly (the A / V already live inside the meter heads). The rheostat name is the only localized text, written directly in the zh / en demos.

## Limitations

| Capability / limitation | Plan | Status / fallback |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dedicated circuit component library | ❌ Won't support | these symbols are custom shapes for this example — good for the low-level hooks, not a large circuit editor |
| Resistor / rheostat drawing as a path generator | 🔧 Optimization | the rectangle and arrow are drawn inside the shape emitter; could move to a [path generator](/core/reference/extending/path-generator) or a dedicated component, left inline here to focus on terminal wiring |
| Electrical validation | ❌ Won't support | retikz handles geometry and rendering, not whether the voltmeter wiring or switch state is physically correct |
