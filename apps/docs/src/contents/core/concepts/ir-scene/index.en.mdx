---
title: IR and Scene
description: retikz has two data layers: IR for declaration and persistence, Scene for rendering.
---

retikz turns JSX into IR first, then compiles IR into Scene. IR preserves what you intend to draw; Scene preserves what a renderer needs to draw.

## Two Layers

| Layer | Role | Audience |
| --- | --- | --- |
| IR | JSON-serializable declaration model that preserves Node / Path / Step / Coordinate semantics | Users, LLMs, persistence, converters |
| Scene | Render-target-neutral primitive list with measured text, paths, and layout bounds | Renderers |

IR is the public contract; Scene is renderer input. You normally do not write Scene by hand.

## Compile Chain

```tsx
<TikZ>
  <Node id="a" position={[0, 0]}>A</Node>
  <Draw way={['a', [80, 0]]} />
</TikZ>
```

The adapter collects this JSX into IR. `@retikz/core` resolves positions, computes anchors, expands paths and bounds, then outputs Scene.

## When to Inspect IR

- Persisting a drawing
- Letting AI generate or patch a drawing directly
- Debugging whether `<ComponentPreview>` outputs the expected structure
- Writing an adapter or converter

Regular component pages focus on the DSL; the complete schema lives in [Schema Reference](/core/reference/schema/scene).
